## Headline findings

1. **The proposed AUD $30k over 3 years is priced at the BOTTOM of the category, not the top.** The nearest strategy-execution comparable with observable transaction data (Workboard) has a median annual contract of ~USD $60,100 with an observed floor of ~USD $28,205/yr - meaning even the *cheapest* observed Workboard deal costs more per year (~AUD $42k) than the entire proposed 3-year contract. AI evidence tools price higher still: AlphaSense ~USD $10-20k *per seat* per year (SMB average ~USD $44,754/yr); Hebbia ~USD $10k/seat/yr (analyst estimates). There is substantial headroom to raise price while staying cheap by category standards.

2. **The non-cancellable 3-year term is the one part of the proposal to drop - it carries real Australian regulatory risk.** Under the unfair contract terms (UCT) regime, a business with fewer than 100 employees OR under AUD $10m turnover is a protected "small business" - which captures many target NFPs and smaller corporates. The ACCC has named non-cancellation clauses, auto-renewals and early-termination fees in small-business standard-form contracts as a 2025-26 enforcement priority, with civil penalties applying since November 2023. (High confidence; primary regulator source. Not legal advice - have an AU lawyer confirm final drafting.)

3. **Transparent pricing is itself a differentiator.** Cascade, Envisio, Spider Strategies, AlphaSense and Hebbia are all quote-only - zero public dollar figures (verified live on all five pricing pages). A solo consultant publishing a clear AUD price gives procurement something the incumbents don't.

4. **Single-tenant "your own instance" is a validated, monetisable premium.** Spider Impact sells four hosting tiers up to customer-hosted on-prem; AlphaSense gates private-cloud hosting + internal-content AI into its premium Enterprise Intelligence tier. Buyers in these segments demonstrably pay for isolation and data control. Most strategy-execution peers are cloud-only multi-tenant, which strengthens the niche positioning.

5. **The differentiation claim survives (directionally).** No verified comparable combines AI-led stakeholder evidence elicitation with a living post-engagement handover. Strategy-execution tools sell seat-based dashboards with no AI interview/evidence layer; AI evidence tools sell premium per-seat research with no engagement/workshop layer. The product occupies the empty intersection. (Medium confidence - this is an absence-of-evidence synthesis across the verified vendor pages, not a feature-by-feature audit.)

6. **The field consolidated:** WorkBoard acquired Quantive (formerly Gtmhub) in May 2025. Drop Quantive from any comparables table; treat its legacy pricing as obsolete.

## Recommended deal structure

Replace the "$5k down + $10k/yr × 3, non-cancellable" shape with a **procurement-standard wrapper carrying the same or better economics**:

- **Year 1: AUD $18-22k** - strategic consulting engagement + implementation + first-year platform licence, presented as one line ("onboarding bundled", the Envisio pattern - the closest NFP-adjacent comparable bundles implementation, training and CSM into the subscription).
- **Years 2+: AUD $10-12k/yr** platform licence - hosting, model currency, support, drift-watch.
- **3-year price-hold** instead of a 3-year lock-in: the client gets multi-year certainty, you avoid the UCT exposure.
- **For-convenience termination** from year 2 (e.g. 60-90 days notice). Envisio-style annual billing; no monthly option.
- **Single-tenant sovereignty as a named premium**, not a silent default - consider a hosted-multi-client entry tier later and let "your own AU-hosted instance" justify the top tier (the AlphaSense/Spider pattern).

Three-year value: ~AUD $38-46k vs the proposed $30k - **higher revenue, lower legal risk, easier procurement conversation**. Your consulting hours stop being an unpriced giveaway and become the named Year-1 engagement.

Hebbia's creator/consumer seat split is a useful packaging idea: consultant-operator seat(s) vs client-viewer seats, if seat-based add-ons are ever wanted.

## What the research could NOT answer (verified claims did not survive)

1. **Pilot / design-partner pricing conventions** - no claims survived verification. Unverified practitioner guidance seen in search (charge 10-30% of first-year value for a pilot, 100% creditable against the contract on conversion) is directionally sensible but uncorroborated. Recommend: paid-but-credited pilots for the first 2-3 design partners, with case-study rights in exchange for the discount.
2. **AU market sizing at the $20-40k band and solo-consultant channels** - no surviving claims. Practical next step is primary validation: the sandbox pilots themselves, plus board-network/AICD-style channels, rather than more desk research.
3. **Multi-year SaaS discount norms** - the commonly-cited "15-25%" benchmark was refuted 0-3. Do not cite it.
4. **Actual AUD quotes from Cascade/Envisio/Spider for a 10-50 seat AU org** - only obtainable by mystery-shopping quotes or buyer references.
